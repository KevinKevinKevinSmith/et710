Mario

Mario is the character that built Nintendo. Does he really need an introduction?

Many elements of his many games are in this edition of Smash. He’s a plumber from Brooklyn with the last name Mario. He jumps on turtles and eats mushrooms. He stars in platformers, RPGs, racing games, and even this fighting game. He’s loaned out to cameos in other games as well. He’s Mario.

In Smash, Mario is a combo fiend. Down throw combos into up tilt, and then later up air. His back air is quick and can stringed into repeated attacks. Then there is his gimping potential. His forward air and spike recovering players, and if that’s too dangerous he has his side special cape and his down special F.L.U.D.D. to reflect and stall people’s recoveries. He has a fireball to interrupt and pressure opponents, and that same cape he can use to block recoveries can also be used to reflect projectiles back at opponents. Mario is just an all round solid character. And just to make him a little bit better, his forward smash has a disjointed sweet spot.

Mario is the average weight brawler. He rushes in, does damage, then jumps out. His recovery is average, but he is great at responding to other players’ recoveries. He has both a projectile and anti-projectile attack depending on the foe. Mario just has a bit of everything and is a very solid character. Good at approaches, good at grab follow ups, good at guarding the edge, good at a lot of things. The only reason to dislike Mario is to want a character that differentiates themselves more.
